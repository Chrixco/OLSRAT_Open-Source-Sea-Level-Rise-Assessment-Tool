  <h2>üåç SLR Vulnerability Mapper</h2>
  <p>
  A QGIS plugin to map <b>flood exposure</b> and <b>urban vulnerability</b> under
  sea-level rise and extreme water level scenarios.
  </p>
  <p>
  The tool integrates:
  <ul>
    <li><b>IPCC AR6 (2021)</b> global mean sea-level projections</li>
    <li><b>FACTS framework (2023)</b> for parametric & structural uncertainty</li>
    <li><b>CODEC return period datasets</b> for storm surge & tide extremes</li>
    <li><b>OpenTopography DEMs</b> and user-provided elevation data</li>
  </ul>
  </p>
  <p>
  Designed for <i>scientists, urban planners, and decision-makers</i>,
  the plugin supports:
  <ul>
    <li>Parcel/building-level risk evaluation</li>
    <li>Integration of social vulnerability indicators</li>
    <li>Evidence-based planning for <b>climate adaptation</b> and <b>resilient cities</b></li>
  </ul>
  </p>
  <p>
  <b>Why use this plugin?</b>  
  It provides a transparent, open-source framework to combine
  <i>sea-level rise projections</i> with <i>extreme sea-level hazards</i>,
  enabling communities to better prepare for coastal futures.
  </p>
